Book Title: The Day the Crayons Quit

Review: I was promised a post-modern exploration of consumer isolation in the style of Proust, illustrating the sorrow of a colorful cast of workaday writing utensils, that, as they edge closer to their inevitable expiration, stage a mutiny--the likes of which the world has never seen. Shamefully, this book is a juvenile attempt at satire, falling far short of delivering on its premise. You'll find yourself wanting to quit long before the crayons do.
